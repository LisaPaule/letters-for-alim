August 29

Day 3 Post-Break Up

It’s day three and it doesn’t seem to be getting any easier. In my head, I always wonder whether you’re thinking of me too in this very moment, whether you miss me and my laugh or my stupid jokes. I think about how just four days ago, we still said good morning to each other and how a week ago, I was so excited to finally see you again after a month. It seems I miss you more now. Because I realise that you’re not here and that you are part of my daily routine.

The uncertainty is killing me. I don’t know what to do. I don’t know whether I should reach out to you already, because I don’t want to not talk to you for a month, but I know you asked for space. I don’t want to invalidate your feelings and force myself onto you, but I don’t want to pull far enough away from you to maybe not be able to find back to each other. I don’t want to wait very long because we don’t have the time to not fix at least some of our issues before November when you have to start studying. I don’t want to not wait because I trust you and I know we need the space. I’m annoyed because it feels impossible to give you the space.

I want to get into the car and drive to Göttingen, force you to speak with me, fix it. But I know it’s the wrong thing to do. I want to give us space to heal and reflect for the future. But it feels like the wrong thing to do. We are stuck between a hard place and a rock. We don’t have many options, but we do have some. I’m sitting and thinking of those, trying to see how one can make them reality and if we’d even be happy, if they became reality, but doing that doesn’t change anything if we can’t talk. I want to be friends and share parts of our lives with one another, but I don’t know whether we can do that without you feeling like I’m constantly trying to rekindle already and without me subconsciously actually doing that.

I’m worried about you and how you’re doing mentally, but I don’t get the right to do that anymore because I lost that privilege. I sit and read through our breakup chat, and I don’t understand how it happened. I don’t understand how I let it happen so carelessly. I don’t know whether you wanted me to fight for us and I didn’t when you needed me to. I know I wanted you to fight for us, but I didn’t give you the time to do that. I know I rushed you and I wish I hadn’t. But I know if I hadn’t, I’d be unhappy now too. I know there weren’t many good options, if any, but I also know that because we gave up, we won’t know what could’ve been if we hadn’t. At least now after breaking up, I know what it feels like not being able to sleep, not being able to eat, constantly having you on my mind, not being able to do nothing. And it hurts. I don’t think anybody deserves to feel like this. And I could’ve told you earlier that this is what it’s gonna feel like, but when you asked me whether we should break up, when you suggested for us to break up, there was no way I could’ve said I don’t want to. There was no way for me to keep you in this unhappy place you were in, force you to stay in a place you were unwilling to be in, a place you thought you’d be hurting me in. But Alim, this hurts.

I wonder whether we’ll get another chance. I hope you were right when you said: ”We will have more than enough chances to hug in the future if we are lucky, but right now we must settle our minds first for a while.” I’m sorry I didn’t reply to you when you said that. I was in my head and trying not to break down packing my things. It wasn’t because I don’t agree. I don’t want to think of an ’if we’re unlucky’-time, and so I won’t, but I just don’t know anything anymore. I want to talk to you, even as just friends, but I can’t because I feel like I shouldn’t, but I want to. I’m torn and it’s the hardest thing I’ve ever had to do. I unarchived you because whenever I open WhatsApp and see a notification in my Archived, my heart jumps and I know that’s gonna become very unhealthy in not too long, so you’re unarchived.

The question I’ll answer today to reflect is: What are specific moments I replay in our relationship? Why those? It’s a question that is hard to answer because in order to answer it I have to actually think about what I’m sad about and not just that I’m sad. I often think about the first moments in our relationship, where we went on dates or we talked. I think about that day after my German IO when we went on a walk to the hospital and you were there for me, you held me while I cried. I think about that day a lot actually. I also think a lot about the pride I felt when I came back down from the stage at Bunter Abend, and how we held hands in the pubes and my heart was skipping out of my chest. I think about how I forgot my keys in your wallet and you came 20 minutes later all the way to my place just to bring me my keys and you weren’t even mad. I think about us laughing when I dropped the pizza in my old place. I think about every time I fell asleep on top of you watching a Fener game, or that one time in March where we were playing against Gala and I was wide awake, complaining about the ref and we were laughing so hard. I think about every time we’d play fight while brushing teeth or wrestle on the bed, and I think about how I beat you every time. I think about how happy you were when I gifted you something, and how happy I was when you gifted me too. I think about how we were happy. Sometimes, I randomly think about that time not too long ago when we died laughing in bed because you said ”Imagine Elias told me he’s pregnant” and how we laughed for 10 minutes straight and after that you said, ”I love your cute laugh” and my heart jumped. Or our bi-weekly competition of ”Can I tell you a secret?” I think about ”Matcha and Missionary”.

And then sometimes I think about a quote of yours you said back in May: ”No one has ever learned their lesson by simply being forgiven for their wrongdoings.” What a loaded thing to say, but a very truthful thing as well. You’re right. And I’m holding on to that. Maybe we would’ve never learned our lesson in what we need to do and change in our relationship, or been urged to solve problems in our surroundings, if we had simply forgiven ourselves and each other for our wrongdoings within the relationship and not been apart. Maybe this is us trying to force ourselves back into the reality that is the consequences of our actions and the limitations put upon us.

It’s day 2 of no-contact and we’ll see how long this goes. Technically, I have another 20 days of that ahead of me, but I’m not sure whether that’s what you want or whether you want me to reach out to you as a friend or just to stay in contact generally. We’ll see. I don’t know when we’ll get to a point where you’ll think I’m clear enough in my head for a conversation about our relationship. What I do know though is that when we do get there and we do talk, it will be in person. That is something I’ve already reflected on. A lot of things would be different, if we had done them in person – including our breakup. But it’s too late now to reminisce over "What could’ve been if...", so I’ll be focusing on "What has to happen in order for...W". So yeah, once again, I love you and till we hug again, Dudu.
