Not every pain spills over.

Not every ache finds its voice.

Some just sit there, stubborn and quiet,

clawing at the edges of your ribs,

waiting for a crack, a shift,

some small mercy to finally let go.

And maybe you've tried.

Maybe you've stood in the rain,

waiting for the sky to shatter first.

Maybe you've curled beneath the covers,

hoping the dark would soften the weight.

Maybe you've listened to every sad song,

but the dam still holds.

And you don't know what's worse—

the pain, or the silence that follows.

So come here.

You don't have to force the flood.

You don't have to break yourself open.

Just let me be here,

in the quiet, in the waiting.

Let me be the warmth against your back,

the hand that holds yours without asking why.

Let me breathe with you,

until your chest feels like your own again.

And if the storm still won't come,

if the sky refuses to give,

then I will stay.

Not to fix, not to push—

just to remind you that

even in the driest, heaviest silence,

you are not alone.
